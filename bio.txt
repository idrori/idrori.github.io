Iddo Drori is an Associate Professor of Computer Science focusing on AGI. He has taught at Stanford, MIT, Columbia University, NYU, and BU with excellent formal evaluations. He has conducted research at Cornell ORIE, MIT CSAIL, and NYU Courant Institute, School of Mathematics, Computing, and Data Science. He was a postdoctoral research fellow in Statistics at Stanford University. He holds a Ph.D. in computer science, an MBA in organizational behavior and entrepreneurship, and has a decade of industry research and leadership experience. His main research is in AGI, AI for superhuman Math and Science, and automated scientific discovery, with over 80 publications and 7,000 citations, and has taught over 60 Computer Science courses. He has won multiple competitions in machine learning, AI, and computer vision conferences and received multiple best paper awards.
